I created the "giving-up" repository as a personal project to document my explorations into changing my food habits along with the ethical considerations that come with it. This isn't just a casual note-taking exercise—it's a structured reflection on my journey toward more conscious eating. 

## What Is This Repo?

At its core, this repository is a collection of thoughts and reflections related to my dietary choices. I'm diving into the impact of those choices—not just on my health but on the environment, animal welfare, and social ethics. I wanted to create a space where I could analyze and articulate why I’m giving up certain foods, and the rationale behind these decisions.

### Why It Exists

Let's face it: food choices aren’t just about taste. They're tied to environmental issues, ethical treatment of animals, and our own health. I wanted a method to track my thoughts and progress as I navigate the landscape of food consumption and sustainability. The project prompts me to critically evaluate:

- Meat
- Dairy
- Fish
- Fast food

Reflecting on these subjects helps me confront my habits and make changes that align more closely with my values.

## Key Design Decisions

### Simplicity is Key

I opted to use Markdown for this project. It’s lightweight, easy to write, and perfect for conveying thoughts without the baggage of a complex CMS. This approach keeps my focus on the content rather than the presentation.

### Static Content

This isn’t a dynamic web app; it’s just static markdown files. I didn't want to get caught up in frameworks or tech that might distract from my purpose. I chose simplicity to ensure that I could concentrate fully on the reflections themselves.

## Stack/Tools

The tools I've used are pretty basic:

- **Markdown** for writing my pieces
- **GitHub** for hosting the repository and collaborating, if needed
- A plain text editor to draft and edit my thoughts

No backend languages or frontend frameworks—just me and my reflections.

## How It Works

### Project Structure

Here's how the repository is organized:

```
/ (root)
  └── index.md      # Main content file with reflections on giving up various foods
```

You can clone the repo to your local machine by running:

```bash
git clone https://github.com/justin-napolitano/giving-up.git
cd giving-up
```

To read the content, open `index.md` with any markdown viewer or text editor. It's as straightforward as that. 

## Tradeoffs

Every design decision has tradeoffs. While simplicity keeps me focused, it also means that richer visualization or interactivity is off the table. I could integrate a database or even a front-end framework for a more engaging experience, but I wanted to keep this personal. Plus, the static format allows me to be candid without the concern of making everything look “perfect.”

Additionally, while my reflections are organized, they could benefit from more sophisticated navigation or tagging. For now, they exist in a straightforward linear format, but that’s a trade I’m willing to accept for simplicity’s sake.

## What I’d Like to Improve Next

Looking ahead, there’s definitely room for growth in the project. Here are a few ideas I’m toying with:

- **Expand Content**: I want to add more detailed reflections about each food category I’m exploring. More depth could result in richer insights.
  
- **Metadata and Tags**: Incorporating structured metadata would help navigate the entries more efficiently. It’d make it easier to find specific reflections or topics.

- **Static Site Conversion**: Turning the repository into a simple static website could enhance presentation and accessibility. A cleaner design might invite more readers into this journey.

- **Resources and Data**: Incorporating external data or resources about ethical food sourcing would bolster the project. Stats about environmental impacts of various foods or links to studies could enrich the dialogue.
